# Product Vision

## One-line definition

ResearchPages is a structured platform for managing a researcher's administrative identity, documents, outputs, and lab-level assets across web and mobile.

## Core problem

Graduate students and professors do not just study and write papers. They operate across:

- multiple affiliations
- multiple funding sources
- project-specific rules
- recurring proposal and scholarship forms
- lab templates and presentation assets
- public-facing profiles and portfolios

Today this is usually spread across folders, Notion pages, scattered files, old self-introduction answers, PPT templates, emails, and memory. That creates repeated writing, missing context, and difficult coordination with advisors or labs.

## Product promise

ResearchPages should make researcher information:

- structured instead of loosely documented
- searchable instead of buried in folders
- reusable instead of rewritten every time
- secure instead of casually scattered
- portable across phone, laptop, and future machines

## Primary user groups

- graduate students
- professors
- lab administrators or research support staff
- research labs as shared workspaces

## First-class product areas

1. Researcher profile vault
2. Affiliation and funding timeline
3. Research plan and application answer bank
4. Paper and output archive
5. PPT and institutional template library
6. Semester timetable manager
7. Lab workspace and shared resources
8. Public profile and lab website generation

## Non-goals for the first release

- full IRIS automation before legal and technical validation
- generalized social networking features
- broad AI features without validated workflow value
- complex desktop packaging before the web app is excellent
